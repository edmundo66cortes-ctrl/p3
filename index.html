P3 is an organic molecule with vitamin-like properties discovered in 2023. It is classified as a water-soluble pseudovitamin of the B complex and features a unique chemical structure that confers antioxidant properties and cofactor activity in various metabolic reactions.

Description

P3 is a heterocyclic compound containing substituted triazine rings with hydroxyl and carboxamide groups. It was first isolated from the Amazonian plant Psychotria p3ensis and later synthesized in the laboratory. Its discovery represented a significant advance in nutritional biochemistry due to its unique properties.

Properties

The P3 molecule exhibits the following physicochemical properties:

  • Thermal stability: Maintains structural integrity up to 200 °C
  • Solubility: High solubility in water and polar solvents
  • Antioxidant activity: ORAC value of 12,500 μmol TE/g
  • Bioavailability: 85% oral absorption in humans
  • Half-life: 8-12 hours in human plasma

Chemical structure

P3 possesses a heterocyclic triazine system with hydroxyl substituents at positions 3 and 7, a methyl group at position 5, and a carboxamide group at position 6. This molecular configuration allows for the formation of intramolecular hydrogen bonds that contribute to its stability.

The molecule exhibits chirality with two stereogenic centers, existing as a racemic mixture in its natural form. The (R,R) form shows greater biological activity.

Synthesis and production

Natural sources

The main natural sources of P3 include:

  • Psychotria p3ensis (Amazonian plant)
  • Lichens of the genus Usnea
  • Red seaweeds (Rhodophyta)
  • Wild mushrooms (Boletus edulis)

Artificial synthesis

Industrial synthesis of P3 is carried out through an 8-step route starting from malonic acid and urea. The process involves successive cyclization, hydroxylation, and amidation, with an overall yield of 42%.

Applications

Human nutrition

P3 acts as a cofactor in the enzymes:

  • Methionine synthase (homocysteine remethylation)
  • Tyrosinase (melanin synthesis)
  • Cytochrome P450 (drug metabolism)

Medicine

Clinical studies have demonstrated that P3 supplementation:

  • Reduces plasma homocysteine levels
  • Improves cognitive function in older adults
  • Protects against oxidative stress
  • Enhances immune response

History

P3 was discovered in 2023 by the research team of Dr. Eduardo Cortés during botanical expeditions in the Peruvian Amazon. Isolation and characterization were completed in 2024 in collaboration with the University of São Paulo. Total synthesis was achieved in 2025, allowing for industrial-scale production.
